About

Hi! I'm Nathan Marquez and I'm currently a sophomore in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University. I've always been excited about technology, and I fell in love with Computer Science upon realizing it's limitless potential for creativity and universal application of problem solving.

Currently, I'm pursuing a B.S. in Artificial Intelligence in order to explore and learn how to use large amounts of aggregated data to create actionable decisions and solutions to the world's most complex problems.

Outside of CS, I love teaching, and have worked as a Sensei at CodeNinjas to teach introductory scripting, digital modeling, and other STEM topics to young children. I'm also passionate about vlogging and videography, and I document my experiences at CMU and give college application advice on my Youtube channel.

Projects

POG PONG

POG is internet slang for “play of the game”, usually denoted to someone who is a skilled pro gamer (“This man is an absolute POG”). POG PONG seeks to challenge POGs through a fun and addicting 3D ping pong game, complete with several levels of AI, an arcade mode, and the option to control the paddle with your hands through OpenCV.
Devpost

Study Buddy

Study Buddy uses a Perceptron Machine Learning Classifier to determine which areas of studying are most impactful for a user. It utilizes openCV to monitor the user as they study. A "focus percentage" is calculated at the end of every study session, and this is fed back into the machine learning pipeline in order to update the user's analytics.
Devpost

Market Spoof Detection/Classification Software (Citi x CMU)

Our mission revolved around "market spoofs", orders placed in order to illegally influence the market. We used PyCaret, a database of ready-made machine learning classification algorithms, to help us build a spoof classifier. We successfully wrote a program in Python to "treat" the data, appending information about the volume and mean order times to each data point.
